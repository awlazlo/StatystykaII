2 The distribution of cases

In this chapter we will see some basic statistisc and information about the distribution of cases in different regions, years and in different age-group.


At first let’s look up the number of new cases of the breast cancer among different genders:

1.All:

## [1] 58118

2.Women:

## [1] 56748

3.Men:

## [1] 1370

It’s obvious from this number that the risk factor for developing breast cancer is female sex. Only 2.36 % from all cases represent men. As we can read in science research breast cancer is more than 100 times more common in women than in men (what can we see from above result).


Next, we want to present the distribution of new cases in different voivodeships and in different years: 2010, 2011, 2012:

We can observe that the number of new cases, in each year, is the largest in voivodeships such as: Mazowieckie and Śląskie. Of course it strongly depends on the number of people who live in each voivodeship, but also there is also big impact of air and traffic pollution.The study links the risk of breast cancer – the second leading cause of death from cancer in women – to traffic-related air pollution. As we can find in GUS (Central Statistical Office) report the most contaminated voivodeships are: Mazowieckie, Śląskie and Wielkopolskie.

From above, we can also add Łódzkie and Pomorskie to the list of voivodeships with the largest number of breast diseases. Both Łódzkie and Pomorskie are also bit polluted voivodeships in Poland.

3 Cartogram

Next step in visualization our data is to use cartogram. It is a thematic map in which areas are shaded or patterned in proportion to the measurement of the statistical variable being displayed on the map, such as population density or per-capita income. In our data we use it to show the number of new cases in counties.

Further considerances refer to female at all ages.

At the beggining let’s consider cartogram for counties. We split up dataset into three years and set 2010 year to plot cartogram. So we have:
